About me

I am currently pursuing a Master's degree in Data Science at the University of Washington, Seattle. Prior to that, I graduated from Purdue University with a Bachelor's degree in Statistics and Mathematics, with a minor in Computer Science. During my time at Purdue, I was honored to receive the prestigious Jean E. Rubin Scholarship, which recognizes exceptional merit among students in the Department of Mathematics.

My interests are centered around the fields of Machine Learning and Natural Language Processing , and I am actively honing my skills in developing and deploying models for production environments. During my undergraduate studies, for my capstone project I worked as a Data Science Researcher on an end-to-end Machine Learning project in collaboration with Caterpillar Inc. Additionally, I worked as a Research Intern in the DURI program at Purdue University, where I contributed to the development of a Deep Learning CNN model for the prediction of extreme weather events.

My interest in Machine Learning was further fueled during my time with the CAM2 research team at Purdue. There, I had the chance to reimplement the YOLO model family using TensorFlow while following software engineering best practices. In my free time I like to write aticles on Medium
